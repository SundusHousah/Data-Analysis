Introduction¶

The collection of data here is about patients not showing up to there medical appointments in Brazil and few questions are going to be answered by investigating this dataset features and finding relationships between them and how are they affected by each other.

Questions To Be Answered¶

  • Whether people with certain chronic disease tend to experience higher rate no show appointments?
  • Which age range tend to miss their appointments?
  • Is contacting patient through SMS help in raising the possibility of showing compare to those who did not receive one?

Conclusions¶

After conducting the data investigation I concluded that a patient with a chronic diseases has almost no effect on the possibility of showing or missing an appointment as only 1% did not show to their appointments. The histogram graphs for Hipertension, Diabetes, Alcoholism, Handcap features proves the week relation with the NoShow feature. On the other had there is a strong correlation between the Age NoShow feature as younger people tend to miss there appointments compare to older ones and we can see that in the histogram graph generated. It seems like communication is key when it comes to minimizing the number of people missing their appointments, as the NoShow mean in respective of messages sent shows that less patients are missing their appointments when they receive a reminder message.
This dataset is easy to work with, it has no duplications and no missing data, the only limitation I had to work with is the fact that I wanted to explore relations with more features, however the non-matching data types prevented me from doing this at this point.
